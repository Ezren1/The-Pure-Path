Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about following the Qur'an alone and abandoning hadith literature.

How do we pray without hadith?

The Qur'an mentions prayer times and basic requirements. The specific movements can be learned from the continuous practice passed down through generations, not from hadith books written centuries later.

"And establish prayer at the two ends of the day and at the approach of the night." ‚Äî Qur'an 11:114

What about the Prophet's example?

The Prophet's true example is preserved in the Qur'an, not in hadith. The Qur'an tells us he followed the Qur'an, was merciful, and had excellent character. That's the example we should follow.

"Say: If you love Allah, then follow me, Allah will love you." ‚Äî Qur'an 3:31

Aren't hadith part of Islam?

Hadith were compiled 200-300 years after the Prophet's death. The early Muslim community followed the Qur'an alone. Hadith became popular later due to political and sectarian influences.

"These are the verses of Allah which We recite to you in truth." ‚Äî Qur'an 45:6

How do we understand difficult verses?

The Qur'an explains itself. Use cross-references, context, and the principle that clear verses explain unclear ones. Allah made the Qur'an easy to understand for those who seek guidance.

"And We have certainly made the Qur'an easy for remembrance." ‚Äî Qur'an 54:17

What about Islamic law (Sharia)?

True Sharia comes from the Qur'an alone. Most of what people call "Islamic law" today is actually based on hadith and human interpretation, not divine revelation.

"And whoever does not judge by what Allah has revealed - then it is those who are the disbelievers." ‚Äî Qur'an 5:44

Will I be accepted by other Muslims?

Truth is not determined by popularity. Many Muslims are beginning to question hadith and return to the Qur'an. Your relationship with Allah matters more than human acceptance.

"And whoever relies upon Allah - then He is sufficient for him." ‚Äî Qur'an 65:3
