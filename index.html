Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about following the Qur'an alone and the issues with hadith literature.

How can we pray without hadith?

The Qur'an contains all the essential elements of prayer. It mentions the times (17:78, 11:114, 20:130), the direction (2:144), ablution (5:6), and the basic movements including standing, bowing, and prostrating (2:43, 22:77).

"And establish prayer at the two ends of the day and at the approach of the night. Indeed, good deeds do away with misdeeds." - Qur'an 11:114

Aren't some hadith necessary for understanding the Qur'an?

The Qur'an explicitly states that it is "clear" (5:15), "detailed" (6:114), and "fully explained" (41:3). Allah would not send an incomplete book that requires external sources for understanding.

"A Book whose verses have been perfected and then detailed from [one who is] Wise and Acquainted." - Qur'an 11:1

Doesn't the Qur'an say to obey the Messenger?

Yes, but the Qur'an defines what "obeying the Messenger" means: following the message he delivered, which is the Qur'an itself. The Messenger's job was to deliver the Qur'an, not to create additional laws.

"The Messenger's duty is only to deliver [the message] clearly." - Qur'an 24:54
